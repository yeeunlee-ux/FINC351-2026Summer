⇄ Lending & Borrowing Mechanism

How individual differences in patience (r) create market interest rates — step by step

① Why trade happens — the PV gap
😌
Patient investor
Personal discount rate: rₚ = 3%
$97.09
how they value $100 in 1 year
Future money feels nearly as good as today's
😤
Impatient investor
Personal discount rate: rᵢ = 20%
$83.33
how they value $100 in 1 year
Future money feels much less valuable than now
Same future $100 — but they value it differently. Patient sees $97.09 today. Impatient sees only $83.33. This $13.76 gap means there is a gain from trade — which is exactly what creates a financial market.
Key formula: PV = $100 ÷ (1 + r)  ·  Patient: $100 ÷ 1.03 = $97.09  ·  Impatient: $100 ÷ 1.20 = $83.33
② The deal — who lends and who borrows?
😌
Patient investor
Values future at $97.09.
Gives up $X today → gets back $X × (1+r*) later.
As long as r* > rₚ = 3%, this is a good deal.
→ LENDS / SAVES
💵→→
market rate r*
←←💰+r
😤
Impatient investor
Values future at only $83.33.
Gets $X now → repays $X × (1+r*) later.
As long as r* < rᵢ = 20%, this is a good deal.
→ BORROWS
Both sides benefit! Patient earns interest above their personal rate (3%). Impatient pays interest below their personal rate (20%). The market rate r* must settle between rₚ and rᵢ for both to participate.
③ Where does market rate r* come from?
Supply of funds
😌
Patient investors save
rₚ = 3%
Will lend at any r* > 3%
Supply increases as r* rises
Demand for funds
😤
Impatient investors borrow
rᵢ = 20%
Will borrow at any r* < 20%
Demand falls as r* rises
r* settles between the two personal rates:
rₚ = 3%
rᵢ = 20%
r* ≈ 10% — where supply of savings = demand for borrowing
This is market equilibrium. No one sets r* from above — it emerges naturally from the interaction of patient savers and impatient borrowers.
④ What if the whole market becomes more impatient?
😌→😐
Patient investor gets slightly more impatient
rₚ rises from 3% → 8%. They save less. Supply of funds falls ↓
📉
😤→🤬
Impatient investor gets even more impatient
rᵢ rises from 20% → 35%. They borrow more. Demand for funds rises ↑
📈
⚡
Result: market rate r* rises
Supply falls + demand rises → r* must increase to restore equilibrium. A more impatient society has higher interest rates.
r*↑↑
This is why countries with high time preference (impatient populations) tend to have higher interest rates. And why central banks care deeply about household saving behavior.
⑤ The law — r is positively related to impatience
📐
The math
PV = $100 ÷ (1+r). Higher personal r → lower PV → future feels nearly worthless → borrow more aggressively → pushes market r* up
🔄
The feedback loop
More impatience → more borrowing demand → r* rises → PV falls further → impatient borrows even more → r* rises again
⚖️
Equilibrium
r* settles where patient investors willingly supply exactly what impatient investors want to borrow. Both personal discount rates shape where r* lands.
Answer to the slide question:
The interest rate r is positively related to investors' time discount rates.
More impatience (↑ personal r) → more borrowing demand → ↑ market r*
