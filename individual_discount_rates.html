🧠 Individual Differences in Discount Rates

Both investors receive $100 in 1 year. Their different personal discount rates (r) reflect their patience — and drive completely different financial decisions.

The core idea: r is not just a market number — it reflects personal psychology. A patient person has a low r (values the future highly). An impatient person has a high r (discounts the future heavily). This difference is what makes lending and borrowing possible.
PV = $100 ÷ (1 + r)  ·  Higher personal r → lower PV → more incentive to borrow now

The r gap (3% vs 20%) creates a $13.76 difference in how they value the same future $100. This gap is the engine of lending and borrowing — and why a market interest rate emerges between them.
Individual differences in r drive financial markets. The patient investor (rₚ = 3%) values $100 in the future at $97.09. The impatient investor (rᵢ = 20%) values the same $100 at only $83.33. This $13.76 gap is the engine of lending and borrowing — and why a market interest rate emerges between them.
Interest rate r is positively related to investors' time discount rates.
More impatient investors → higher r → lower PV → more borrowing
The market rate r* settles between rₚ and rᵢ — where supply of savings meets demand for borrowing
💡 Class discussion: What is YOUR personal r? Think about it — if someone offered you $100 today vs $103 in a year, would you wait? What about $100 today vs $120 in a year? The rate where you'd be indifferent is your personal discount rate!
