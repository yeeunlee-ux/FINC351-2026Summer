Yield Spread & Credit Spread

Corporate bonds must offer a higher yield than Treasuries of the same maturity to compensate investors for default risk. The gap is the yield spread. When that gap is purely due to default risk, it is called the credit spread.

Recovery Rate

Even if a company defaults, bondholders usually recover some fraction of face value through asset sales or restructuring. This fraction is the recovery rate. What's lost is called Loss Given Default (LGD).

How credit spread and recovery rate connect

A higher probability of default OR a lower recovery rate → wider credit spread demanded by investors.
