Visual explainer: credit spread, yield spread, and recovery rate on bonds

Yield spread — the extra return investors demand

Corporate bonds must offer a higher yield than Treasuries of the same maturity to compensate for default risk. The gap between them is the yield spread. The portion due purely to default risk is the credit spread.

Recovery rate — what you get back if the issuer defaults

Even if a company defaults, bondholders usually recover some fraction of face value — through asset sales, restructuring, or liquidation. This fraction is the recovery rate.

How credit spread and recovery rate connect

A higher probability of default OR a lower recovery rate → wider credit spread demanded by investors.
